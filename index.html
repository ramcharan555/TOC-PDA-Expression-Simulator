Why a Pushdown Automaton (PDA)?

Regular finite automata cannot handle nested parentheses — they have no memory. A PDA adds a stack, giving it infinite memory for the current nesting depth. This makes it powerful enough to recognize all context-free languages, including valid arithmetic expressions.

Operator Precedence Rules
^Exponentiation — highest precedence (right-associative)
* /Multiplication & division — medium precedence
+ −Addition & subtraction — lowest precedence
( )Parentheses — force grouping, not treated as operators

The stack ensures higher-precedence operators are output (and thus evaluated) before lower-precedence ones — without writing any explicit comparisons in the grammar. This is the PDA's power: the stack encodes the precedence structure implicitly.
